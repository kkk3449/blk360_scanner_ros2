# Journal figure set — occlusion-aware visibility stop-scan

All figures are regenerated by `scripts/render_journal_figs.py` from the recorded
run JSONs, saved maps and trajectory CSVs. R = 6 m, τ = 0.30, A_min = 5 m² unless
noted. "LOS coverage" = fraction of mapped free space with guaranteed
line-of-sight from at least one scan pose (union of ray-cast visibility regions).

**Fig. 1 — Coverage model.** A single BLK360 scan pose on the three-room map.
(a) The isotropic disk B_disk(s,R) counts every cell within R as covered, bleeding
through the partition and perimeter walls. (b) The proposed ray-cast visibility
region B(s,R) stops at the first occupied/unknown cell along each ray, so only
genuine line-of-sight area is claimed. Motivates the whole method.

**Fig. 2 — Proposed stop-scan, single room.** Visibility stop-scan on the BLK360
test-room (Cartographer SLAM map + robot path + per-scan visibility polygons,
scan order #1…#n). 4 scans, 97.9 % LOS coverage.

**Fig. 3 — Range sweep.** Placement and coverage for R = 5 / 6 / 10 m. Larger R
needs fewer scans but spreads them; R = 6 m balances coverage (97.9 %) against
inter-scan overlap for registration. Used to justify the operating point.

**Fig. 4 — Multi-room, live comparison.** Identical sim configuration, only the
coverage model differs. (a) The disk model skips candidates that lie within R
(straight line) of a prior scan through the partitions → 2 scans, 46.9 % LOS,
a whole room unscanned. (b) The visibility model scans once per room → 3 scans,
95.1 % LOS. Qualitative headline result.

**Fig. 5 — Controlled skip-rule ablation.** Deterministic, confound-free: the same
environment map and the same candidate path (logged trajectory sampled every 2 m)
are replayed under both skip rules, so the *only* difference is the decision.
(a) Single-room and (b) multi-room. The disk rule is structurally capped at 2–3
scans and leaves occluded pockets/rooms (≈74–87 % LOS); the visibility rule fills
them (94–95 % LOS).

**Fig. 6 — Coverage completion.** Optional phase: after frontier exploration, the
robot drives into each still-uncovered free-space pocket with Nav2 and scans
(★) until none ≥ min_pocket_area remains. Here 3 frontier + 3 completion scans,
99.6 % LOS — "no white left."

**Fig. 7 — Summary statistics.** Disk vs visibility skip rule replayed on N = 5
real trajectories each, paired per path. (a) LOS coverage, (b) scan count
(mean ± s.d.). Single-room: 77.0 ± 4.9 % → 85.4 ± 6.2 %. Multi-room:
77.0 ± 6.4 % → 84.0 ± 9.2 %. Per-path gain up to +21–23 %p on full-traversal paths.

**Fig. 8 — Parameter sweep.** Deterministic replay over the same N = 5 paths per
environment while varying the skip thresholds (LOS = solid ●, left axis; scans =
dashed ■, right axis). (a) τ sweep at A_min = 5 m²: coverage and scan count are
flat across τ ∈ [0.10, 0.50] (≈2 %p), so τ = 0.30 sits in a robust region.
(b) A_min sweep at τ = 0.30: A_min is the operative coverage-vs-scans knob;
A_min = 5 m² is the knee — coverage is within ~5 %p of the aggressive A_min = 1 m²
setting (90.7 %) at roughly half the scans (3.6 vs 7.2). Justifies the
operating point (τ = 0.30, A_min = 5 m²).
